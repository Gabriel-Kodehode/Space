Nexus


population: 622,408
Gravity: 1.7g
Diameter: 160mil
Ecosystem: Deserts, Under ground neon cities


Nexus is a scorching desert planet with vast dunes and blistering winds, its surface barren and desolate. Beneath the sand lies a hidden world: sprawling neon-lit cities, glowing with vibrant hues of electric blues, pinks, and greens. These underground metropolises thrive, powered by advanced geothermal systems. Sleek, metallic structures rise within massive caverns, interconnected by high-speed maglev trains. Neon signs and holograms illuminate the streets, casting a futuristic glow on the bustling nightlife. Water is extracted from deep aquifers, while hydroponic farms sustain the population. Nexus’s underground cities pulse with life, a stark contrast to the desolate world above.

Stonevault


population: 16.232.348
Gravity: 0.3g
Diameter: 201mil
Ecosystem: Stone lands


The planet Stonevault is a barren, rocky world, with an inhospitable surface scorched by its sun and lashed by violent winds. Its people live underground, in vast, interconnected caverns carved from the planet's stone layers. These subterranean cities are illuminated by bioluminescent plants and powered by geothermal energy. The stone walls provide natural insulation from the harsh surface conditions, and intricate tunnels connect homes, markets, and community spaces. Water is extracted from deep underground reservoirs, and food is grown in carefully managed hydroponic farms. Life below the surface is a blend of resourcefulness and resilience, hidden from Stonevault’s hostile exterior.

Greenhell


population: None
Gravity: 6.3g
Diameter: 2800mil
Ecosystem: Uninhabitable


Greenhell is a massive gas giant, with swirling, stormy clouds of deep, toxic greens, teals, and blacks, giving it an ominous, almost malevolent appearance. Its atmosphere is thick with poisonous gases like methane, ammonia, and sulfur compounds, which contribute to the eerie, noxious shades of green. Towering storms constantly rage across the planet’s surface, with lightning flashes illuminating the dark, swirling clouds. The storms are so intense that they span thousands of miles, their powerful winds and corrosive rain a perpetual, violent force.
